## 3. Gamma Environment Breakdown: Low Net GEX vs High Net GEX

The total Net GEX background value defines whether the market-maker environment will actively fight the trend or completely get out of its way.

### A. Low Net GEX Context (Ultra-Low Friction)
* The Option Physics: Total Net GEX prints at historically low values relative to standard sessions (e.g., remaining under 1.0 or dropping from morning highs). This confirms that the option chain has almost entirely lost its stabilizing, price-pinning power.
* The Price Interaction: On high Net GEX days, positive gamma forces MMs to sell futures as the price climbs, creating an institutional "brake pad." Under Low Net GEX, this brake pad is entirely removed. MMs do not need to fight hard against the upward move.
* The Result: Price drifts, floats, and grinds slowly upward with zero resistance. Because the overhead book is empty of negative options friction, it requires very little organic buying volume to systematically carry the asset higher into the vacuum.

### B. High Net GEX Context (High Friction)
* The Option Physics: Total Net GEX prints at highly elevated positive values, indicating a dense concentration of outstanding options positioning across the front-month grid.
* The Price Interaction: The market-maker stabilizing engines are fully engaged. As the asset tries to expand, MMs flood the ask side of the book with heavy futures limit supply to dynamically offset their expanding long gamma profiles.
* The Result: The market becomes highly rigid and sticky. The upside is heavily capped, turning any attempted breakout into a grind that is highly prone to stalling out at the nearest horizontal options strike.
